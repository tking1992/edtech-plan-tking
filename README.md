# Educational Technology Plan

The purpose of this Educational Technology Plan is to design a practical and inclusive technology plan for a middle school Digital Literacy & Computer Science course. The plan identifies the hardware, software, network infrastructure, and classroom technologies needed to support student learning. It also explains how these tools will be used to teach computer science concepts, support digital literacy, provide feedback, and create meaningful learning activities. In addition, the plan considers evidence-based learning practices, student access, inclusion, ethical and legal responsibilities, and possible challenges such as limited devices, internet issues, or changes to online or hybrid instruction.

## Context

### Course

**Computer Science & Digital Literacy**

This course is designed to help students become confident, responsible, and creative users of technology. Students will learn digital citizenship, as well as foundational computer science concepts, including problem-solving, coding, and online safety. Through hands-on projects and collaborative activities, students will use technology to create, communicate, think critically, and better understand how computers and digital tools shape the world around them. 

Digital Literacy & Computer Science eill be taken by all 7th and 8th grade students. Once in 7th grade and once in 8th.

### Situation

The course addressed in this Educational Technology Plan is a middle school course titled "Computer Science & Digital Literacy." The course is designed for 7th and 8th grade students and will be taken twice (once in 7th grade and once in 8th grade). Because of this aspect, the course needs to be flexible enough to support students of varying levels of experience. Some students will be focused on learning basic computer science concepts for the first time, while others will have already been introduced to to coding, digital-citizenship, or other technology-based projects. With this in mind, the course should include foundational skills, as well as offer opportunities for extension, creativity, and deeper problem-solving. 

The school context strongly influences the technologic decisions made in this plan. Students will likely be relying on school-issued Chromebooks to complete assignments, access coding platforms, digital lessons, and other various online resources. This is important because it allows the course to use web-based tools such as Google Classroom, as well as other browswer-accessible resources without requiring extensive software installation. The use of Chromebooks also makes it easier for students to access teh same materials across different classrooms and learning environments.
